Guitar Boost Pedal

When playing an electric guitar in a band, there comes a time when you want to cut through the mix, whether that’s during a solo, or during a heavy riff. A clean boost pedal is a great tool in these scenarios, which simply amplifies the signal slightly without introducing any artifacts such as overdrive or distortion (unless the boost pedal is before an overdrive pedal in the signal chain).

I decided I wanted to build my own, and found a popular design online called the AMZ MOSFET booster, which is a very simple amplifier circuit that can be made using a BS170 N-channel MOSFET, a few resistors/capacitors, and a potentiometer to control the gain. All these components can be found easily in through-holes packages, and can be assembled on a small piece of vero/matrix board.

After soldering all the components onto vero board, I mounted is inside a die cast aluminium Hammond box, along with a footswitch for on/off control, a blue status LED, a 9V barrel-jack connector for power, two TRS sockets for signal in/out, and a panel-mount potentiometer for gain control.

After wiring everything up and powering it on, the pedal was able to provide a subtle volume bump on low gains, and could significantly increase the volume to the point of mild distortion as the potentiometer was turned up. I added some labelling using coloured sheets of Vinyl, cut using a CNC plotter that my college had.
